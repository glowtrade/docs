## Perpetual Futures

Glo Exchange offers **perpetual futures** (perps) — derivative contracts that let you speculate on an asset's price without owning the underlying asset. Unlike traditional futures, perps have no expiry date.

### Key Concepts

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Long vs Short">
    - **Long**: You profit when the price goes up. Open a long if you're bullish.
    - **Short**: You profit when the price goes down. Open a short if you're bearish.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Leverage">
    Leverage amplifies your position size relative to your margin. With 10x leverage and $100 margin, you control a $1,000 position. Higher leverage means higher potential profit *and* higher risk of liquidation.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Margin">
    Margin is the collateral you deposit to open a position. All positions on Glo Exchange are margined in **USDC**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Funding Rate">
    Perpetual futures use a **funding rate** mechanism to keep the contract price aligned with the spot price. When the perp trades above spot, longs pay shorts. When below spot, shorts pay longs. Funding is exchanged every 8 hours.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Liquidation">
    If the market moves against your position and your margin ratio falls below the maintenance margin, your position will be **liquidated**. Use stop-losses and appropriate leverage to manage this risk.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Order Flow

When you place a trade on Glo Exchange:

1. Your order is signed by your embedded wallet (via Privy)
2. The order is submitted to Hyperliquid's on-chain orderbook
3. Hyperliquid's matching engine fills your order against existing liquidity
4. Your position is reflected on-chain and in your dashboard

<Info>
  All trades are settled on Hyperliquid's L1 blockchain. Glo Exchange never holds custody of your funds.
</Info>
